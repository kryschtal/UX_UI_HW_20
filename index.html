MORSEL

Overview


Adult men and women want to improve their eating habits, but find it difficult to track what they are eating throughout the day. They want to be able to see how balanced their diet is.


Morsel is a simple interface designed for the iOS of a smartphone and smartwatch to track the intake of the major food groups. It helps users to visualize how balanced their diet Is on a daily, weekly, and monthly basis. `


Over the course of 2 weeks and along with one team member we both conducted research, created a prototype, conducted usability test and iterated based on those findings.


We used Google Suite, Adobe XD, and InVision during this project.

01 Research


Initial Research

Our competitor analysis showed us early on that trackers were typically too complicated requiring more than 15 minutes on average to put in one meal. Some also lacked historical data. We conducted 5 semi-structured 30 minute interviews and received 14 responses to a survey sent out.



Results

Majority of our users considered themselves generally healthy but still wanted to improve their diet. Having a balanced diet was what was most important to them, with the goal of living a long and active life. 29% of our survey respondents prefer using a tracking method to keep them accountable.


02 Definition

Define

From all the research we determined that our main users would be people over the age of 20. They were not looking for a dieting app, but rather finding a tool to help them track their eating habits to notice trends and missing gaps.


Persona

We then created a user persona highlighting someone who needed to use the tool to manage their dairy intake due to health impacts. This helped us better understand not only to track daily goals but also figure out what happens when you go over your goals.



03 Ideation


Define

The initial app design was created for the smartwatch. We proceeded this way because there was a lot of features and data we wished to ensure were available and conveying that on a small screen with limited tools and buttons was needed to be determine early on.


Persona

There was concern about showing visual representation when something was added to the tracker along with how to properly show the monthly goals. These were addressed with additional research into a variety of exercise tools that track things such as steps over the course of a month. Though in this case it was showing data for 6 different things rather than just steps/distance.

06 Conclusion


There are many things we found successful in this project. We had created a simplified interface for users to track their food focusing on their food group intake rather than calories to show how balanced the users diet was.


The major steps going forward would be to flush out was is a proper serving size and to teach the users how to incorporate that into their daily tracking.


We would also want to include badge functionality along with create proper push notifications that won’t deter the users.


Finally it would be important to allow the users more control over their icons they are using and their order. If a vegetarian or vegan are using this tool it is unlikely they would want a big icon of a steak to help represent protein.
